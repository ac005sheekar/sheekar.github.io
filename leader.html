MindSparks 2020

Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology

Finalist
Team at AUST
Project Setup

We demonstrated an Environment Monitoring System with Machine Learning based Predictive Features at MindSparks'20, AUST. This time Saleh Bin Sayeem led our team "SENSO CODER" and I served as second in command. We developed a complex monitoring platform on Arduino ATMega with various sensors and implemented a Random Forest Algorithm for predictive data analysis.

Technical Implementation

Developed complex circuitry on Arduino ATMega with sensors (Sound, DHT11 temperature-humidity, dust, CO2 and YL69 pH). Implemented a library with Random Forest Algorithm for predictive data analysis within the platform.

IUBAT Intra-University Robotics Contest

IUBAT University

Organizer & Participant

As the founder and Chief Trainer of IUBAT CSE Robotics Club, I organized and participated in multiple intra-university robotics contests. These events served as training grounds for national competitions and helped develop the robotics culture at IUBAT.

Impact & Legacy

These intra-university competitions helped train approximately 200 students on robotics systems, 40+ different sensor types, and programming. The trained teams went on to win 5 National Technological Project Competitions within 3 years of the club's establishment.

Competition Impact & Legacy

From Competition Failure to Research Success

After our team's elimination in the quarter-finals of Robo Carnival 2019 at BUET, I transformed this setback into a research opportunity. The experience led to the development of "Nano Rover" - a multi-sensory surveillance robot with modified inception-net.

Nano Rover Phase-1 Demo Research Paper Best Paper Award
Key Achievement

This research transformation demonstrates the ability to convert competition challenges into successful research outcomes, resulting in a Best Paper Award at a Springer Nature affiliated international conference in 2021.
